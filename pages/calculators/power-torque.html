Practical guidance

Engineering Notes

◇

Mechanical power & torque

Size drives for peak duty, starting torque, service factor, and thermal limits—not nominal power alone. Torque is the rotational force that causes rotation, measured in N·m or lb·ft. Power is the rate of doing work, measured in watts or horsepower.

△

Speed & angular velocity

Guard rotating shafts and couplings. Verify overspeed, shaft stress, and emergency-stop requirements. Angular velocity (ω) relates to RPM by ω = 2πN/60. Always check critical speed to avoid resonance.

!

Motor & gearbox selection

Do not mix kW with W, rpm with rad/s, or input power with useful shaft output. Select motors based on full-load torque, not just power rating. Consider service factor for shock loads.

η

Shafts & industrial duty

Well-sized industrial motors commonly operate most efficiently around 75–100% of rated load. Shaft diameter must withstand torsional stress, deflection, and critical speed requirements.

⚙

Gearbox & transmission design

Gearboxes transform torque and speed according to the ratio. Output torque = input torque × ratio × efficiency. Account for losses in bearings, gears, and seals when sizing.

◎

Industrial applications

Common applications include conveyors, pumps, compressors, fans, and machine tools. Each has unique torque-speed characteristics. Variable frequency drives (VFDs) enable precise speed control.
