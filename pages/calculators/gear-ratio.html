Engineering Equations

Engineering Assumptions

  • External spur gears mesh with a single point of contact and a constant pressure angle.
  • The driven gear rotates at the calculated ratio based on ideal tooth geometry.
  • Efficiency losses are applied to transmitted torque and power only.

Worked Example

Given

Driver teeth = 20, driven teeth = 60, driver speed = 1750 rpm, driver torque = 120 Nm, efficiency = 95%.

Formula

Gear ratio $i = N_{driven} / N_{driver}$ and $T_{out} = T_{in} \times i \times \eta$.

Substitution

$i = 60 / 20 = 3$ and $T_{out} = 120 \times 3 \times 0.95 = 342$ Nm.

Answer

The gear train reduces speed by a factor of 3 and increases torque to approximately 342 Nm at 95% efficiency.

Engineering Notes

Typical applications

Motor reducers, conveyor drives, robotics, machine tools, and automotive transmissions.

Design considerations

Check contact ratio, tooth bending stress, lubrication, backlash, and shaft loading.

Efficiency considerations

Higher ratios and lower-quality lubrication can reduce transmitted efficiency and increase heat generation.
